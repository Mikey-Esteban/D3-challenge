Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

In respects to poverty, there is no real discernable difference amongst the populations lacking health care, the populations obesity or smoking population.

In analyzing health factors with age, the youngest state (Utah) did have the lowest percentage of smokers; while the third oldest (Wyoming) by far and away and the highest percentage (26.7%). By and large median age had no defining qualities in relation to the 3 health factors.

Obesity and smoking had a very small decline in overall percentage as the average household income rose. Surprisingly income did not seem to lower the lack of health care among states.
